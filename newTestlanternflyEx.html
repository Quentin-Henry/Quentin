Lanternfly

Graphic Design, 2023

Overview

The Lanternfly Project explores how aesthetic confusion can be weaponized in public discourse through an invasive species crisis. The project mirrors corporate tactics of using visual ambiguity to paralyze public action, drawing parallels to how tobacco companies and oil companies have historically muddied urgent issues through sponsored messaging. The Spotted Lanternfly, arriving from Southeast Asia during COVID-19, serves as both metaphor and mascot for examining institutional manipulation and xenophobia.

Typography Guidelines

  • Use Bookman for headlines and major display text to convey institutional authority
  • Employ Gotham for body copy and UI elements to maintain readability and modern appeal
  • Maintain clear hierarchy through consistent size relationships
  • Consider the tension between fonts as part of the narrative

Color Usage

The color palette reinforces the project's themes of tension and ambiguity:

  • Red represents both danger and attraction
  • Black and white create stark institutional contrast
  • Beige provides a neutral ground that suggests both natural and bureaucratic contexts

Design Application

Layout Principles

  • Use generous white space to create institutional authority
  • Employ grid systems that can be intentionally broken
  • Layer information to create visual confusion when appropriate
  • Balance institutional rigidity with organic forms

Visual Hierarchy

  • Lead with Bookman headlines for institutional weight
  • Use Gotham for clarity in detailed information
  • Employ color strategically to guide or confuse
  • Create tension between order and chaos in layouts

Process

The project's visual language plays with tension: between 'old' and 'new' New York in its typography (Bookman vs. Gotham), between institutional authority and approachability in its mascot, and between beauty and destruction in its subject matter—reflecting complex narratives of invasion, immigration, and public response during crisis.
